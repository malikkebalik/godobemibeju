Louis Vuitton was a French fashion designer and the founder of the world-renowned luxury goods company bearing his name. He was born on August 4, 1821 in Anchay, France to his parents, Emily and Mallet Vuitton, who worked as farmers, and Louis was the youngest of their five children.

At the age of 13, he was apprenticed to a box-maker and packer in his hometown. He worked in the box-making business for 17 years, and during this period, he developed the skillset that would eventually make him famous.

In 1837, Vuitton moved to Paris, the capital of fashion and luxury goods, where he began designing handbags and trunks.
