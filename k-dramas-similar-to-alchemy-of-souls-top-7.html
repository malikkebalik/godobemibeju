K-Dramas Similar To Alchemy Of Souls (Top 7)

Publish date: 2024-09-16

Fantasy K-drama, Alchemy of Souls left a good impression on fans with its amazing story of magic, love, and dark secrets.

If you’re a fan of casting spells and true romance, then this is a series that is worth watching. The characters are always trying to help each other because of their twisted fate with the undead. Along with romance and magic, there is always betrayal to try and stop good things from happening to the two lovers.

If you liked Alchemy Of Souls, you might love these 7 KDramas. In this article, RNN will feature K-dramas similar to Alchemy Of Souls that will definitely interest you.

Top 7 K-Dramas Similar To Alchemy Of Souls

  • Bulgasal Immortal Souls     (16 episodes)
  • Arthdal Chronicles        (18 episodes)
  • The Scholar Who Walks At Night     (20 episodes)
  • Lovers Of The Red Sky    (16 episodes)
  • Gu Family Book   (24 episodes)
  • A Korean Odyssey (20 episodes)
  • Diary Of A Night Watchman  (24 episodes)

1. Bulgasal Immortal Souls

Bulgasal is an amazing story very similar to Alchemy Of Souls, starring Lee Jin-Wook and Kwon Nara is a drama that has incorporated various elements of different genres such as romance, fantasy, supernatural, sci-fi, and revenge. The story here, 1000 years in the making, centers on a guy named Hwal, a man-turned-immortal cursed since birth who vows revenge on a woman with a mysterious past and who happens to be responsible for his immortality and miseries.

Also, The series tells the story of a woman who remembers her past lives by repeating death and reincarnation for 600 years, and a man who cannot kill nor be killed and has lived for the past 600 years as a Bulgasal a mythical creature that feeds off human blood and is cursed with immortality. It aired on tvN from December 18, 2021, to February 6, 2022. It is also available for streaming on Netflix.

2. Arthdal Chronicles

This is another K-Drama similar to Alchemy Of Souls, It is a story of mythical heroes, their struggle, unity, and love of people living in a virtual land called Arth. Set in the fictional land of Arthdal during ancient times. Eun Som was born with a fate to bring disaster to Arthdal. Due to his mother’s struggles to save him, he goes through hardships and grows up.

The inhabitants of the ancient city of Arthdal contend with power struggles, while some encounter love along the way. Eun-seom goes through hardships to bring his people back to life and learns of his true origins in the process. This series is Regarded as the first Korean ancient fantasy drama, the story takes place during the Bronze Age and is loosely based on the story of Dangun, the founder of the first Korean Kingdom of Gojoseon.

The series aired on tvN from June 1 to September 22, 2019, on Saturdays and Sundays at 21:00 (KST) for 18 episodes. It premiered internationally on Netflix.

3. The Scholar Who Walks At Night

Scholar Who Walks the Night is a fantasy historical K-drama, this series is about a scholar named Kim Sung Yeol (Lee Joon Gi) who becomes involved in a plot to take down the evil vampire Gwi (Lee Soo Hyuk) which results in Sung Yeol becoming a vampire himself. He dedicates his life over the next 120 years to find a way to destroy Gwi.

What could be better than a historical drama with a vampire destined to fight another vampire? That is the gist of the next drama on the list The Scholar Who Walks The Night. The story is tragic, it is beautiful and “bloody” entertaining. While we have two vampires with tragic pasts who are destined to be enemies, the plot can sometimes get chaotic. You would have either love or hate situation with no in-between so there is no pressure to watch it, but do give it a try. The historical aspect of it is strong and the show has got some pretty good costumes that you will like.

However, he is haunted by the death of his first love, especially when he meets her doppelganger, Choi Hye Ryung (Kim So Eun). It aired on MBC from July 8 to September 10, 2015,  for 20 episodes.

4. Lovers Of The Red Sky

This is another interesting K-drama similar to Alchemy OF Souls, this is a love story featuring ghosts, demons, and gods. Hong Chun Gi (Kim Yoo Jung), a painter of extraordinary talent, is born blind due to a curse but later gains vision. She is accepted into a prestigious art school despite societal views on such pursuits for a woman.

Set during the Dan dynasty era when ghosts, demons, and gods are involved in human life, the story recounts the fateful relationship between a female painter (Kim Yoo-Jung) and a blind astrologer (Ahn Hyo-seop). It begins at the sealing ceremony conducted in the 3rd year of King Seongjo’s rule where they try to extract Ma Wang (Demon King: god of Death), who is residing in former King Yeongjong’s body, and seal it inside a portrait of the King painted by a divine painter.

It premiered on SBS TV on August 30, 2021, and aired every Monday and Tuesday at 22:00 (KST) for 16 episodes. Is a must-watch Korean historical drama. I loved every bit of it. The cast, their acting, their costume, and everything.

5. Gu Family Book

This series is another fantastical kind of K-Drama, Gu Family Book (Korean: 구가의 서; RR: Guga-ui Seo; also known as Kang Chi, the Beginning) is a 2013 South Korean television series starring Lee Seung-gi and Bae Suzy.

Gu Family Book is a historical fantasy about a half-human/half-gumiho named Choi Kang Chi (Lee Seung Gi). Kang Chi’s mother, Seo Hwa (Lee Yeon Hee), was a human and his father, Wol Ryung (Choi Ji Hyuk), was a gumiho. Their tragic love story ended with Kang Chi being raised by another family, unaware of his true nature. He eventually falls for a girl named Yeo Woo (Suzy) and discovers his true identity. He then struggles to hold on to his humanity in the midst of the turmoil that befalls his family.

Gu Family Book is not so recent, the series was aired on MBC from April 8 to June 25, 2013

6. A Korean Odyssey

Adding to the fantastic K-Drama similar to Alchemy Of Souls, the Hong sisters the writers of Alchemy of souls wrote 2017, A Korean Odyssey, a south Korean fantasy tv series.

A Korean Odyssey (Hwayugi) is a romantic fantasy drama. It stars Lee Seung Gi as Son Oh Gong, an immortal monkey spirit, and Oh Yeon So as Jin Seo Mi, a woman with second sight. Jin Seo Mi is actually Sam Jong, who is destined to save the world.

Having made a contract with Seon-mi 25 years ago, entitling her to seek help from Son Oh-gong whenever she calls him in exchange for letting him free, the two meet again in a fateful encounter.

It aired on tvN starting December 23, 2017, every Saturday and Sunday at 21:00 (KST).

7. Diary Of A Night Watchman

This is another Historical K-Drama similar to Alchemy Of Souls, Diary Of A Night Watchman is a 2014 South Korean television series starring Jung Il-woo, Ko Sung-hee, Yunho, and Seo Yea-ji. It aired on MBC from August 4 to October 21, 2014, for 24 episodes.

This is Set in the Joseon dynasty, one who denies ghosts, who seeks to use them, and one who seeks to destroy them vie for power in this light-hearted, romantic, fantasy action. Prince Rin found himself an orphan when his father the king, under a spell, killed the queen and then take his own life. Considered unsuitable for the throne, the child prince was sent away from the palace and his older half-brother Gi-san, son of a concubine, was crowned the new king of Joseon.

Twelve years later, Rin, now grown up, spends his days between fun and amorous encounters, hiding the loneliness he feels and his ability to see ghosts, acquired after escaping the attack of a spirit when he was a child. One day, Rin meets Do-ha, a young shaman from Mount Baekdu searching for her older sister. Besides Do-ha, a Taoist priest, Sadam, arrives in Hanyang, chosen by the king to expel an evil spirit that haunts him, actually a result of the sovereign’s madness.
