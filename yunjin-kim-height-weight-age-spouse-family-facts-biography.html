Yunjin Kim is a South Korean actress and theater artist who is known for her television roles like playing Sun-Hwa Kwon in the ABC science fiction drama series Lost, Jang Hee-ju in the drama series Love in 3 Colors (유정), Karen Kim in the mystery drama soap opera series Mistresses, Seon Woo-jin in the heist crime drama series Money Heist: Korea – Joint Economic Area, and Ms. Ma in the mystery revenge drama series Ms. Ma, Nemesis. In addition to that, she has also been cast in many other shows and movies which include the likes of The Legend of Gingko, Shiri, Yesterday, Ardor, Diary of June, Seven Days, The Neighbor, Harmony, Heartbeat, House of the Disappeared, Confession, Splendid Holiday (화려한 휴가), Foreboding (예감), Iron Palm, Mother, Rush!, Ode to My Father, and Pawn.

    Yunjin Kim Facts

  • In 1980, Yunjin Kim and her family immigrated to the United States.
  • When she was in grade 7, the young Kim enrolled in her middle school drama club and performed in the My Fair Lady musical.
  • She is a trained ballet, jazz, Afro-Caribbean, and Peking opera dancer.
  • Yunjin is a trained fighter and knows different fighting styles known as Quarter Staff, Broad Sword, Taekwondo, and Unarmed.
  • The actress has also released XOXO, a Japanese photobook.
  • She speaks both Korean and English fluently.
  • Kim represented Korea as a Goodwill Ambassador for the 2002 World Cup.
  • In 2006, Maxim ranked her at #98 on the Hot 100 list.
  • She is popularly known as The Korean Julia Roberts.
  • She played Yoo Ji-yeon (a lawyer and mother) in the 2007 crime thriller film Seven Days.
  • Yunjin was seen in the South Korean ballad trio SG Wannabe’s Timeless music video.
  • In the past, Yunjin had auditioned for the role of Izzie Stevens on the drama series, Grey’s Anatomy. However, the role was eventually played by Katherine Heigl.
  • Alongside Jung Woong-in, Ko Sung-hee, and CNU, she co-starred as a mystery writer named Ma Ji-won in the revenge mystery television series Ms. Ma, Nemesis in 2018. For this role, she was nominated for an SBS Drama Award in the “Top Excellence Award, Actress in a Daily and Weekend Drama” category.
  • In 2022, she started playing the role of Seon Woo-jin (a crisis negotiation team leader belonging to the National Police Agency in South Korea) in the heist crime drama series Money Heist: Korea – Joint Economic Area. It is the 2nd series in the Money Heist franchise and stars Yoo Ji-tae, Park Hae-soo, Jeon Jong-seo, Lee Hyun-woo, Lee Won-jong, and Park Myung-hoon.
