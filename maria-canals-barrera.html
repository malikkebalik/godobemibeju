Maria Canals-Childhood, Barrera’s Biography, and Education

Maria Canals-Barrera was born on September 28, 1966, in Los Angeles, California, USA. Her parents are of Catalan origin. She is of American nationality and has a mixed ethnic heritage.

Maria received her bachelor’s degree from the University of Miami. She is also conversant in Spanish and studied acting in junior high.

Professional Career of Maria Canals-Barrera

Maria Canals-Barrera began her acting career in theater, performing in both Miami and Los Angeles. She made her professional debut in the Spanish-language telenovela Marielena on Telemundo in 1992. She made her big screen debut the following year in Cop and a Half, which was an instant box office success, generating over $41 million against a production budget of $4 million.

Barrera rose to prominence after starring as Theresa Russo in the Disney Channel family sitcom Wizards of Waverly Place from 2007 to 2012, opposite David DeLuise, Selena Gomez, David Henrie, and Jake T. Austin. She later appeared in the films Camp Rock (2008), Camp Rock 2: The Final Jam (2010), and Larry Crowne (2011).
