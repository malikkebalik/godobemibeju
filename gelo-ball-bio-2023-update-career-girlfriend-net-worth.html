Publish date: 2024-07-13

Early Life & Family

Gelo Ball was born in Anaheim, California, the United States, to LaVar Christopher Ball and Tina Slatinsky. His father is a former football player and businessman.

LaVar is usually criticized for his parenting style and is often labeled as controlling. Besides that, he met his wife, Tina Slatinsky, in college.

Tina is a former basketball player for the Cal State Golden Eagles. She survived a severe stroke that left her with aphasia.

Moreover, the entire family stars in a Facebook reality television show called Ball in the Family.

Together the duo has three sons, namely LiAngelo, Lonzo, and LaMelo. Along with LaVar, Tina plays a significant role in their sons’ basketball careers.

LiAngelo started training and working out when he was four years old. His father trained him, alongside his brothers, from a very young age.

He played against fifth graders and his brothers when he was in kindergartner and also played flag football at 7.

As a high school student, he had an exceptional basketball career at Chino Hills High School. He played for four years with the high school team.

Later, he enrolled at California University, Los Angeles, and in his freshman year, he committed to the UCLA team.

Gelo Ball | High School & College Career

Gelo played basketball before he could even write his name. Since childhood, he wanted to be a professional basketball player. The G League shooting guard played high school basketball for Chino Hills Huskies.

He aided the Huskies to their first-ever CIF Southern Section Open Division. In his junior year, he led the Huskies to the City of Palms Classic championship with his brothers.

To sum up, he had a 35-0 record with the Chino Hills team in 2016, and MaxPreps named him the mythical national champion.

Gelo had an outstanding career in his senior season. He scored a career-high 72 points that year in a win against Rancho Christian School.

Subsequently, he gathered much-deserved attention and praise for his game. Several media outlets and newspapers named him the All-Area Player of the Year.

He was also the first-team All-State and honorable mention All-American selection by MaxPreps.

College Career And China Incident

After high school, he attended the University of California, Los Angeles. He played a few games, including pre-season games. His journey with the college didn’t last long.

UCLA conducted its 2017-18 season in Shanghai, China. During the regular-season opener, he was arrested alongside two of his teammates.

The shooting guard stole sunglasses from a Louis Vuitton store in a mall near their hotel. LiAngelo would have faced serious prison time and fines.

However, he was released from custody. Nonetheless, the college suspended him as a punishment for his actions. After a good discussion with his father, the small forward decided to withdraw from UCLA.

Gelo Ball | Professional Basketball Career

Prienai And Los Angeles Ballers

Before joining the NBA G League and serving the Oklahoma City Blue, he was linked with basketball club Prienai of the Lithuanian Basketball League (LKL). He decided to prepare for the 2018 NBA draft while playing in the LKL.

Gelo’s younger brother was also playing for the club. After that, the brothers built an excellent reputation as Prienai players and participated in various games and events. Their family sports apparel company, Big Baller Brand, sponsored the boys.

Shortly after, he declared his participation in the 2018 NBA Draft. Many draft experts predicted his chances of getting drafted were slim.

While playing against an outstanding LKL team, Gelo injured his ankle and soon left the basketball club. Later, his father publicly criticized the head coach.

Furthermore, he went undrafted in the 2018 NBA draft.

Then he played for the Los Angeles Ballers in the Junior Basketball Association (JBA). LaVar was the founder of JBA, which serves as an alternative to college basketball.

LiAngelo flourished in that environment and explored his true potential. He was the co-MVP of the JBA All-Star Game.

Moreover, he won the All-Star Game MVP award and was the Finals MVP. He was also named To the JBA USA select Team (2018).

The shooting guard suffered an ankle injury, that required surgery, leading him to miss most of the 2019-20 season.

Oklahoma City Blue

The former Prienai player joined the NBA G League team Oklahoma City Blue at the end of 2019. He was with the team on a waiver claim. Ball was ready to show the world his true potential.

However, the pandemic resulted in the cancellation of the 2020 season. The Oklahoma team played a game before the cancellation, but LiAngelo did not appear in the game.

Detroit Pistons

On December 2, 2020, Gelo was signed to a training camp contract by the Detroit Pistons. However, on December 13, he was waived without playing in the December 11 pre-season game.

The American basketball player was invited by his brother LaMelo to work out with the Charlotte Hornets in the summer of 2021. After his impressive workout session with the team, he was signed by the Hornets on October 14, 2021. However, he was waived the next day.

Greensboro Swarm

The Greensboro Swarm selected LiAngelo as the 14th overall pick in the 2021 NBA G League draft on October 23, 2021. In his debut for the Swarm, he scored 22 points.

Ball signed a non-guaranteed contract with the Hornets on September 26, 2022, but was waived on October 15, 2022.

Later, on October 23, he joined the Greensboro Swarm roster for training camp.
