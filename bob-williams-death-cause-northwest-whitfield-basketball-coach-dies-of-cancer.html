Cancer kills Northwest Whitfield coach Bob Williams

Bob Williams, the senior girls’ basketball coach at Northwest Whitfield High School, fought a valiant battle against cancer but succumbed to the disease on Thursday.

His untimely death has left a profound absence in the souls of his students, faculty, and the entirety of the Northwest Whitfield community.

Before joining Northwest Whitfield, Bob Williams earned a reputation as the boys’ basketball coach at Walker Valley High School.

He carried his experience and enthusiasm to his new position, instilling discipline, cooperation, and tenacity in his players.

His coaching technique transcended gender distinctions, as he subsequently led both the boys’ and girls’ basketball teams to the state tournament as head coach for McMinn County.

Even while confronting illness, Bob Williams continued to be a driving force on the sidelines, providing leadership and motivation for his team.

In June 2022, at the Best of Preps banquet, he was awarded the Bobby Davis Award for Heart and Desire in recognition of his unwavering commitment.

This award represented his tenacity and dedication to instructing despite his ongoing treatment for Stage 4 cancer.

Bob Williams will be remembered not only for his coaching accomplishments but also for his genuine concern for and support of his athletes.
