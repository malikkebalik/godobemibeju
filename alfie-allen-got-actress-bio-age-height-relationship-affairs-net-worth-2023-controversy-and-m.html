Alfie Allen (GOT): Bio

Alfie Allen is an English actor who was born on September 12, 1986. He is best known for playing Theon Greyjoy in Game of Thrones, which airs on HBO (2011-2019).

Allen was born in Hammersmith, London. His parents, Alison Owen, who was a film producer, and Keith Allen, who was from Wales, had him. Lily Allen, who is his older sister and a singer, wrote the song “Alfie” about him. He went to Windlesham House School in Sussex, Embley Park School near Romsey, St. John’s College in Portsmouth, and the Fine Arts College in Hampstead, where he got his A-levels. He is also Sam Smith’s third cousin once removed.

Allen’s first job was in a one-off Channel 4 comedy called “You Are Here,” which was written by Matt Lucas and David Walliams and aired in 1998. Their mother made the movie Elizabeth, which Allen and his sister Lily were in the same year.

In his early work, he had small parts in films like Agent Cody Banks 2: Destination London, which was directed by his uncle Kevin Allen, the movie Atonement, and the BBC1 drama Casualty 1907, in which he played Nobby Clark. On January 31, 2008, in Chichester, he took over Daniel Radcliffe’s role in a revival of Equus that was going on tour across the country.

In April 2009, he was in the music video for “Dust Devil” by Madness with his then-girlfriend Jaime Winstone. He also played a part in Freefall, a BBC2 movie.
